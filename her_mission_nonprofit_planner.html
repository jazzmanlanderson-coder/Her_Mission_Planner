Her Mission Nonprofit Planner

A complete guide to starting and running a nonprofit serving women, children, and families in need — from first steps to full operation.

📍 McLean County, Illinois
🏗

Phase 1 — Foundation (Months 1–3)

Start here
1

Define your mission & vision

Write a clear 1–2 sentence mission statement. Who do you serve? How do you serve them? Keep it focused — domestic violence survivors, foster children, runaway youth, and families in need through mentorship, drives, and outreach.

2

Choose a name & check availability

Search the Illinois Secretary of State business registry (ilsos.gov) and the USPTO trademark database. Make sure your name is unique and available as a .org domain.

3

Recruit founding board members

Aim for 3–7 board members. Seek women in law, finance, social work, education, and community organizing in McLean County. Illinois requires a minimum of 3 directors for a nonprofit corporation.

4

File Articles of Incorporation with Illinois

File with the Illinois Secretary of State. Cost is $50. You can file online at ilsos.gov or in person at 1510 W Market St, Bloomington. Choose "Not-for-Profit Corporation."

5

Draft and adopt bylaws

Bylaws govern how your org operates — board meetings, voting, officer roles, and conflict of interest policies. Illinois requires bylaws. Use the Illinois Attorney General's nonprofit resources for free templates.

📋

Phase 2 — Legal & Tax Status (Months 2–4)

6

Apply for your EIN

Free and instant at IRS.gov. Required before opening a bank account or filing for 501(c)(3) status. Apply online — you'll get your number immediately.

7

File IRS Form 1023 or 1023-EZ

This is your federal tax-exempt status application. 1023-EZ (for orgs expecting under $50k/year) costs $275 and is faster. Full 1023 costs $600. Approval typically takes 3–6 months.

8

Register with Illinois Attorney General

Illinois law requires registration with the IL AG Charitable Trust Bureau before soliciting donations in the state. File Form CO-1 (charitable organization registration). Annual renewal required.

9

Open a dedicated nonprofit bank account

Never co-mingle nonprofit and personal funds. Bring your EIN, Articles of Incorporation, and bylaws. Heartland Credit Union and local banks in Bloomington-Normal are good options.

10

Get Directors & Officers (D&O) insurance

Protects your board members from personal liability. Nonprofit liability insurance is especially important when serving vulnerable populations like children and DV survivors.

🚀

Phase 3 — Launch & Grow (Months 4–12)

11

Build your online presence

Create a website with a donation button. Apply for Google Ad Grants ($10,000/month in free ads) and Canva for Nonprofits. Set up social media — Facebook and Instagram are key for McLean County community reach.

12

Launch your first program

Start with one school supply drive or one mentorship cohort. Partner with YWCA Labyrinth or the Baby Fold rather than duplicating existing work. Document everything for future grant applications.

13

Apply for grants & build your donor base

Begin with Illinois Prairie Community Foundation and United Way of McLean County. Build relationships before submitting formal applications. Start a monthly donor program early.

14

Establish community partnerships

Connect with YWCA Labyrinth (DV), Baby Fold (foster care), Salvation Army Safe Harbor (runaway youth), McLean County schools (supply drives), and Mid Central Community Action (family services).

Fundraising & Outreach
Domestic Violence Support
🛡 Women's Safety & Recovery Mentorship

Partner with YWCA Labyrinth (Bloomington's existing DV resource center) to fill gaps rather than duplicate services. Focus on what they don't already offer.

  • 1-on-1 mentorship matching with trained community women
  • Financial literacy and budgeting workshops
  • Job readiness coaching and resume help
  • Safe housing navigation and referrals
  • Childcare connections during recovery period
  • Safe messaging and trauma-informed communication training for mentors
Foster & Runaway Youth
🏡 Youth Stability & Mentorship Program

Partner with the Baby Fold (Normal) for foster youth referrals and Salvation Army Safe Harbor for runaway youth. Youth aging out of foster care are especially vulnerable in McLean County.

  • MENTOR-certified mentor matching model
  • Life skills: budgeting, cooking, job searching, renting
  • Emergency supply kits: hygiene, clothing, school supplies
  • Safe space drop-in hours and peer support groups
  • Connection to Heartland Community College programs and scholarships
School Supply Drives
🎒 Back-to-School & Year-Round Supply Drives

McLean County has two school districts — BPS 101 (Bloomington) and Unit 5 (Normal). Partner with school counselors to identify highest-need students.

  • Partner with local churches, businesses, and Walmart/Target for drop-off sites
  • Create an Amazon wishlist and share on social media
  • Coordinate with BPS 101 and Unit 5 district counselors
  • Expand to winter coat drives, hygiene drives, and holiday toy drives year-round
  • Ask Heartland Community College student groups to volunteer
Community Outreach
🏘 Family Resource Fairs

Host quarterly fairs that bring services directly to families — housing, food, healthcare, legal aid, and childcare all in one place. McLean County has strong agency partners ready to participate.

  • Partner with Mid Central Community Action, FCRC, Salvation Army, and YWCA Labyrinth
  • Offer free food, children's activities, and door prizes to boost attendance
  • Collect intake forms to build your service database
  • Invite WGLT, Pantagraph, and local TV news to grow awareness
  • Consider locations: Bloomington Public Library, Salvation Army, or church community rooms

Illinois has specific nonprofit requirements beyond the federal IRS process. Make sure to complete all state steps — especially the AG registration, which is often missed by first-time founders.
Illinois-Specific Formation Steps
1

File as an Illinois Not-for-Profit Corporation

File Articles of Incorporation under the Illinois General Not For Profit Corporation Act (805 ILCS 105). Cost: $50. File at ilsos.gov or in person at 1510 W Market St, Bloomington.

2

Minimum 3 Directors Required

Illinois requires at least 3 directors on your board. They do not need to be Illinois residents but should be trustworthy community members committed to your mission.

3

Register with the Illinois Attorney General

File Form CO-1 with the IL AG Charitable Trust Bureau before soliciting ANY donations. Cost: $15 initial + $15/year renewal. This is required by the Solicitation for Charity Act. Visit illinoisattorneygeneral.gov.

4

Annual Report to Illinois Secretary of State

File an annual report each year before the anniversary of your incorporation. Cost: $10. Required to maintain your active status as an Illinois corporation.

5

Annual Report to Illinois Attorney General

File Form AG990-IL every year once registered with the AG. Due 6 months after your fiscal year end. This is separate from the Secretary of State annual report.

6

Mandatory Reporter Training

Illinois law requires anyone working with minors to complete mandated reporter training. All volunteers and staff in your youth programs must complete this before working with children. Free training at dcfstraining.org.

7

Illinois Sales Tax Exemption

Once you have 501(c)(3) status, apply for Illinois Sales Tax Exemption with the IL Department of Revenue (Form STAX-1). Saves money on supplies, equipment, and event purchases.
